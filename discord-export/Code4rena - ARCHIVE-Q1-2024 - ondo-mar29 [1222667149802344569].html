thomassb1625 03/30/2024 18:08
Yea apologies for the natspec being wrong. As you're not the first to ask about this, I'll just walk through the whole rOUSG flow here for anyone else to reference. So let’s say ousgPrice = 100, and a user wraps 1 OUSG, so 1e18 (as OUSG, rOUSG, and the rOUSG price all have 18 decimals of precision). This will correspond to 1e18 * OUSG_TO_ROUSG_SHARES_MULTIPLIER = 10_000e18 shares. If we view their balance of rOUSG, it will be (shares * ousgPrice) / (1e18 * OUSG_TO_OUSG_SHARES_MULTIPLIER) = (10_000e18 * 100e18) / (1e18 * 10_000) = 100e18 rOUSG = 100 rOUSG. Now let’s say price increases to 105, now they will have: (10000e18 * 105e18) / (1e18 * 10000) = 105 rOUSG. If a user instead deposited 4 OUSG at first, they’d now have 420 rOUSG. Or, say another user deposits 1 OUSG after the price increase, they would still receive 105 rOUSG. 1 rOUSG is essentially just $1 of OUSG (edited)
